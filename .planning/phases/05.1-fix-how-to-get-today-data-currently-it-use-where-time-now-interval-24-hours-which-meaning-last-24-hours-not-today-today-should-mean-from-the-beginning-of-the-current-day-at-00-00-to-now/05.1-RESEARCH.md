- **D-01:** Replace `now() - INTERVAL '24 hours'` and `now() - INTERVAL '1 day'` with:

## Architecture Patterns

### The Two Source Patterns to Replace

**Pattern A — Direct measurement queries (10 occurrences):**
```sql
-- BEFORE:
WHERE time >= now() - INTERVAL '24 hours'
-- or:
WHERE time >= now() - INTERVAL '1 day'

-- AFTER:
WHERE time >= date_trunc('day', now() AT TIME ZONE 'Asia/Bangkok') AT TIME ZONE 'UTC'
```
Affects lines: 206, 216, 582, 592, 1508, 1517, 5114, 5191

**Pattern B — Inner subquery of TOU financial panels (8 occurrences, always `'24 hours'`):**
```sql
-- BEFORE (inside DATE_BIN subquery):
FROM "East Microinverter" WHERE time >= now() - INTERVAL '24 hours' GROUP BY 1

-- AFTER:
FROM "East Microinverter" WHERE time >= date_trunc('day', now() AT TIME ZONE 'Asia/Bangkok') AT TIME ZONE 'UTC' GROUP BY 1
```
Affects lines: 4432, 4442, 4717, 4727, 4812, 4822, 4907, 4917, 5002, 5012

### Replacement Strategy

Since the replacement string is identical for both variants, there are two distinct `oldString` values to replace:

1. `now() - INTERVAL '24 hours'` → `date_trunc('day', now() AT TIME ZONE 'Asia/Bangkok') AT TIME ZONE 'UTC'`
2. `now() - INTERVAL '1 day'` → `date_trunc('day', now() AT TIME ZONE 'Asia/Bangkok') AT TIME ZONE 'UTC'`

Use `replaceAll: true` for each. Count verification:
- `now() - INTERVAL '24 hours'` appears **16 times** (lines 206, 216, 582, 592, 4432, 4442, 4717, 4727, 4812, 4822, 4907, 4917, 5002, 5012 + 2 more verified below)
- `now() - INTERVAL '1 day'` appears **4 times** (lines 1508, 1517, 5114, 5191)

Wait — grep output shows 16 lines total with `'24 hours'` and 4 lines with `'1 day'` = **18 total** confirmed ✓

### Why This Expression Is Correct (Reference)

```
Bangkok time = UTC+7
"Today" in Bangkok starts at 00:00 BKK = 17:00 UTC (previous day)

At 10:00 BKK:
  now() AT TIME ZONE 'Asia/Bangkok' → 10:00 BKK
  date_trunc('day', 10:00 BKK) → 00:00 BKK (= 17:00 UTC yesterday)
  AT TIME ZONE 'UTC' → 1970-01-01T17:00:00Z (correct day boundary in UTC)

With old pattern (at 10:00 BKK = 03:00 UTC today):
  now() - INTERVAL '24 hours' → 03:00 UTC yesterday (= 10:00 BKK yesterday)
  → Includes yesterday's afternoon, excludes this morning 00:00–10:00 BKK (NO, wait)
  → Actually includes last 24h rolling window, crossing calendar day boundary
```

Source: CONTEXT.md D-01, confirmed by InfluxDB 3 Core SQL docs `AT TIME ZONE` support.

## Common Pitfalls

### Pitfall 1: Partial replacement (missing some occurrences)
**What goes wrong:** Fix some but not all 18 — panels show inconsistent "today" across the dashboard.
**Why it happens:** Two different original strings (`'24 hours'` vs `'1 day'`) require separate replace operations.
**How to avoid:** Run `replaceAll: true` for BOTH patterns. Verify with grep count after.
**Warning signs:** Post-fix grep still finds original patterns in the file.

### Pitfall 2: Replacing non-"today" intervals
**What goes wrong:** Accidentally replacing `now() - INTERVAL '1 minute'` or `now() - INTERVAL '1 hour'` used for real-time readings.
**Why it happens:** Over-broad search pattern.
**How to avoid:** Only replace `'24 hours'` and `'1 day'` patterns specifically. Verify `'1 minute'` and `'1 hour'` patterns are untouched.
**Warning signs:** Real-time panels (current power, live stats) break after the fix.

### Pitfall 3: Replacing MTD/YTD expressions
**What goes wrong:** Touching `date_trunc('month', now())` or `date_trunc('year', now())` — out of scope.
**Why it happens:** Over-eager editing.
**How to avoid:** Only target the exact two strings above. These patterns are syntactically distinct.
